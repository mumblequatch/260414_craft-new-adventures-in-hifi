New Adventures in Hi-Fi

by Thomas Woodrow

My six-year-old son, Benjamin, has been obsessed with podcasts for a good while. Sharing one with him started as an idea for helping him get to sleep, but of course listening to an exciting tale from Greek mythology energized and woke him up instead. That use case was a fail, but overall the introduction was a success: now he listens in the car, in the bath, on the Bluetooth speaker wherever we may be. What can I say, the kid loves stories.

Having just completed an illustrated book for him, I thought: “hey, why not a podcast? I’ve made movies. Podcasts are just audio. Should be easy.”

Cut to: me, six months later, still wrestling with technical issues involving gain staging, mic positioning, EQ’ing, mix translation, and commenting to anyone who will listen: “this is the hardest thing I’ve ever tried.” (In fairness, for several of those months I was dealing with, in sequence, a defective interface, a defective mic, and defective headphones — just supernaturally bad luck.)

Along the way, I learned A LOT. Mic technique. Room acoustics. Signal processing (built a chain from scratch in the end). I even built software to automate parts of the production pipeline because the tools available didn’t work the way I wanted. This is to say nothing of figuring out how to actually speak — and perform — into a microphone.

It’s both incredibly challenging and also incredibly satisfying to do it all as a one-man-band. It’s virtually free (apart from the time invested) and you get to learn about a million different things along the way. Best of all, you get to share a new, immersive story with your kid, who can’t get enough of them.

Anyway, if you’ve gotten this far, thanks for reading. And if any of this is interesting to you, I’m always happy to nerd out.
